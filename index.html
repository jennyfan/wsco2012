Welcome

On behalf of The Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania and the Wharton Supply Chain Organization, we are delighted to welcome you to the inaugural Wharton Global Supply Chain Conference. We are proud to host this first of its kind conference at Wharton that will disseminate crucial operational know-how.

Behind every successful corporation is an outstanding supply chain. This conference will show us how ten of the world's finest enterprises operate.

The kick-off theme "Optimize to Thrive: Incorporating Innovation to Transform your Enterprise", will witness some of our favorite firms sharing the best practices and innovative strategies that make their supply chains truly outstanding. Our speakers are Senior Vice Presidents and Vice Presidents who have transformed some of the world's largest supply chains and we salute them for such remarkable managerial ability. They will offer their personal insights on how to optimize with the supply chain to succeed in unpredictable times.

With the panels representing as many as 7 industries of Hi-Tech, E-commerce, Pharmaceuticals, Aerospace & Defense, Chemicals, Consumer Products, and Retail, this conference brings forth the world of cutting edge operations right here at the Wharton School.

We invite you to take home this exclusive knowledge, build new relationships, and begin to lead the next chapter of operational innovation.

Panels

Panel 1

High Technology

Chips, components, panels. How are these assembled in the best possible way to build some of the decade's most outstanding PCs & smart-phones? (e.g. what went into the making of the Samsung Galaxy S3). On the other hand, Google delivers search results as fast as possible at the lowest potential cost owing to its specialized infrastructure of fiber-networks, switches, servers & storage devices. We will explore the strategies adopted by our panelists to manage and optimize on these gigantic supply chains (e.g. acquisitions, in-house manufacturing) and get a feel of the architecture of the hi-tech future.

  • Jim Miller, VP, Worldwide Operations, Google
  • Tim Carroll, VP, Global Operations - Integrated Supply Chain, IBM
  • Kevin Geklinsky, Director of Business Planning, Samsung

Panel 2

Innovate to Thrive

Three different industries - one common strategy. This panel will show when, where and how innovation can be applied in the supply chain. It will also address one of today's most pressing issues - transferability of supply chain innovation concepts across industries. We will also explore their individual supply chain models and our panelists will share the challenges they foresee in their respective sectors. With Chemicals, Consumer Goods, and Aerospace & Defense being the major growth sectors, this panel offers crucial insights towards tracing the present and future state of the global economy.

  • Don Wirth, VP, Global Operations - Corporate Supply Chains, DuPont
  • Randall Lovorn, VP, Commercialization & Innovation Strategy, PepsiCo
  • Kenneth Shaw, VP, Supply Chain Management, Boeing
  • Ken Murtha, Sr. VP Global Supply Chain & Strategy, Astrazeneca

Panel 3

E-Commerce : Today & Tomorrow

This panel will demonstrate the operating models and recent supply chain challenges of the world's largest retailer and the world's largest shoe retailer. The focus point is how Amazon & Zappos.com position their main asset, the supply chain, to maintain competitive advantage and keep their customers incredibly happy. We will explore the major components from start to finish - their excellent vendor relations, cutting edge inventory & warehousing (including Zappos.com's robots), efficient transportation & distribution, and extraordinary customer service. Finally, how can these best practices be imparted in the larger retail industry and smaller e-commerce start-ups?

  • Mike Roth, VP, North American Operations, Amazon
  • Craig Adkins, VP, Service & Operations, Zappos.com
  • Noah Ready-Campbell, Cofounder & CEO, LikeTwice.com

James (Jim) Miller

Google Inc.: VP, Worldwide Operations

Jim Miller is vice president of Supply Chain Operations at Google. In this role, he has responsibility for global operations, planning, supply chain and new product introduction for Google's IT infrastructure.

Prior to joining Google in 2010, Jim worked with leading companies in electronics, networking, clean tech and communications technology, manufacturing and consumer services. His expertise in supply chain, both management and consulting, was honed at Amazon.com, Cisco, First Solar, Sanmina-SCI Corporation, IBM Corporation, Intel and Sierra Crest Consulting.

Jim has a bachelor's degree in aerospace engineering from Purdue University, and master's degrees in mechanical engineering and management from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

Jim serves on the advisory boards of a number of West Coast clean tech and supply chain software start-ups and the Leadership Council of the Corporate Eco Forum. He is also on the Industrial Advisory Board of Purdue University's School of Aeronautical and Astronautical Engineering, where is he is a recipient of the Outstanding Aerospace Engineer Award.

Tim Carroll

IBM: VP, Global Operations - Integrated Supply Chain

Tim Carroll is Vice President, Global Execution for IBM's Integrated Supply Chain. Named to this position in March 2011, he is responsible for driving consistency and focused execution for all aspects of Supply Chain operations worldwide, including Supply/Demand, Inventory Management, Client and Channel Enablement, Client Advocacy, and Solutions optimization across seven IBM business units. Prior to this Mr. Carroll was Vice President, Global Operations for IBM's Integrated Supply Chain, serving in that role since 2005.

Mr. Carroll joined IBM in 1981 and has held numerous executive positions in a variety of roles, including brand sales and worldwide manufacturing, distribution, and fulfillment for IBM's personal computer business. In 2005, Mr. Carroll was assigned as the senior executive responsible for executing the divestiture of IBM's personal computer business to Lenovo. This was the most complex divestiture in IBM's history, shifting 11,000 employees across 22 business functions in 66 countries in less than 4 months.

He is a member of the Integrated Supply Chain leadership team that is focused on continuous improvements in organizational capabilities to meet the demands of an increasingly interconnected and global environment. He is also a member of IBM's Innovation and Leadership Team.

Mr. Carroll currently serves on the Board of Directors of the WakeMed Foundation, the charitable arm of WakeMed Health & Hospitals which supports the health system in its commitment to providing quality healthcare to all citizens of the community.

Mr. Carroll has a bachelor's degree in Information Science from the State University of New York College at Oswego.

Don Wirth

DuPont: VP, Global Operations - Corporate Strategy

Donald D. Wirth is vice president - Global Operations, Corporate Supply Chains. He joined DuPont as a project manager in 1981 in Sydney, Australia

He then held plant and site manager roles in Explosives and Imaging. In 1989, he was appointed general manager - Operations for DuPont Australia. In 1992, he was transferred to Singapore as operations director - Agriculture Products. In 1999, he moved to the United States as Six Sigma Champion for the Agriculture Enterprise. In 2001, he was appointed global operations director - DuPont Crop Protection. In 2006 he was named as vice president - Operations, Agriculture & Nutrition. In July 2007 he was named vice president of Global Operations - Excellence, Center of Competency to lead implementation of the DuPont Production System. He was named to his current position in October 2009.

Prior to joining DuPont, Mr. Wirth worked with W.E.B Consulting from 1975-1981 as a consulting Engineer and associate.

He received a bachelor's degree in chemical engineering from the University of New South Wales in 1974.

Randall Lovorn

PepsiCo: VP, Commercialization & Innovation Strategy

Randall Lovorn is a 22 year veteran of PepsiCo. Through his career, he has worked in all domestic divisions of PepsiCo. He has held numerous roles across Supply Chain working in multiple disciplines including Warehouse, Transportation, Planning, Strategy, Information Systems and Commercialization.

Currently, Randall serves as the Vice President of Beverage Commercialization and Independent Bottler Integration. In this role, he is responsible for working closely with Marketing and Sales to bring new product and packaging innovation to the market place. His scope covers all brands of soft drinks as well as the broader beverage portfolio that PepsiCo has to offer -including Pepsi, Mountain Dew, Sierra Mist, Lipton Teas, Starbucks Coffee, SoBe and Aquafina Waters. In addition, Randall is responsible for working with PepsiCo's Independent Bottler Network to drive broader distribution of our innovative products while optimizing our total Blue System network. Currently, Randall is accountable for the successful launch of nearly $800MM in new products annually. During his tenure in this role, he has led the redesign of the cross-functional innovation process, created a regional innovation approach, and worked with Franchise bottlers to create a more effective blue system network.

Kenneth Shaw

Boeing: VP, Supply Chain Management, Global Services & Support

Kenneth A. Shaw is vice president of Supply Chain Management for the Global Services & Support business within Boeing Defense, Space & Security. In this role, Shaw is responsible for developing and leveraging strategic supplier relationships for all Global Services & Support programs while overseeing an organization with more than 1400 employees at 10 sites across the United States, Australia, and the United Kingdom that provides Supply Chain Management services for more than $4 billion of material annually.

Prior to his role in Global Services & Support, Shaw served as director of Supplier Management & Procurement for Boeing Space & Intelligence Systems - Special Programs, where he focused on developing and implementing supplier management strategies to improve program execution and supplier performance, and foster business growth opportunities.

Shaw has held positions of increasing responsibility in supplier management and procurement across Boeing's defense and space programs since joining the company in 1992. He led Supplier Management & Procurement for Integrated Defense Advanced Systems business, where he focused on growing the business portfolio by leveraging supplier technologies and relationships.

Prior to that assignment, Shaw led the supplier management and procurement team supporting the Homeland Security and Services organization with five offices across the United States and two offices in Australia. The organization procured more than $800 million in the first six months of its inception, across a broad spectrum of products and services. The team delivered more than $100 million in savings to its programs through innovative supply chain management strategies and developed and deployed the small business strategy that was recognized by the Department of Transportation with its inaugural Prime Contractor of the Year award in 2002 for accomplishments on the Airport Explosives Detection System program.

With more than 20 years of experience in supplier management in the aerospace industry, Shaw began his career in major subcontracts at Northrop Corporation's B2 division. In 1992, Shaw moved to Boeing (formerly McDonnell Douglas) to join the C-17 military airlifter program. He was instrumental in the C-17 supplier cost reduction program that resulted in more than $1 billion in supplier savings. He also was responsible for leading both the Avionics/Flight Controls and Aircraft Systems Supplier Management organizations on the C-17 Program, managing more than 80 major subcontractors in five countries. Additionally, he led the Outside Manufacturing and Strategic Contracting organization across multiple Southern California sites, supporting programs for both government and commercial customers, with a focus on the development and execution of a comprehensive supply base management strategy.

Shaw holds a Master of Business Administration (MBA) degree from the Marshall School of Business at the University of Southern California. He has a bachelor's degree from the University of California, Los Angeles.

Ken Murtha

Astrazeneca: Sr. VP, Global Supply Chain & Strategy, Global Operations

Mr. Murtha is the Sr. Vice President, Global Supply Chain & Strategy, accountable for the design of supply chain strategies for commercial brands and for the mid-term planning of all commercial supplies at the global level. He is the key interface to R&D for the launch of new products and responsible for directing and overseeing the delivery of all major strategic investments such as ERP and Lean as well as all IS systems.

In 1999, he assumed the role of Vice President, Supply Chain Operations and Strategy for AstraZeneca, accountable for leading the operational and regulatory activities associated with the sourcing, planning and supply chain management of all US commercial products. In addition, he was accountable for the development of and delivery of all strategic programs associated with the US Operations function. He provided executive leadership as a member of the US Operations leadership team and member of the US Commercial portfolio team. Later, he assumed the role of Vice President of Business Operations in the United States before assuming the role of Regional Vice President of Supply Americas, including all operational responsibility in North and South America. In January 2010, he assumed my current role of accountability for Global Supply Chain & Strategy for AstraZeneca worldwide.

Prior to Astrazeneca, he began his career in 1982 with the Merck manufacturing division. In 1992, he became one of the original founders of AstraMerck. He was Head of Operations for AstraMerck and Astra Pharmaceuticals.
